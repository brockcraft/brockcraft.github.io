About this Dashboard

Methodology

This dashboard summarizes an ongoing evaluation of the HCDE BS curriculum against ABET EAC Criterion 5b, which requires a minimum of 45 semester credits (SC) of engineering topics, including engineering design, engineering science, and modern engineering tools.

Each course is evaluated by examining its Canvas course export (assignments, rubrics, syllabi, and module structure). Credit estimates are expressed as quarter-credit (QC) ranges — a low estimate reflecting only the most clearly ABET-legible content, and a high estimate including content that qualifies with reasonable interpretation. Quarter credits are converted to semester credits using the standard UW conversion factor (×0.667).

Credit estimates reflect the portion of a course's content that qualifies under Criterion 5b — not the course's full credit value. A 5 QC course may contribute only 1–2 QC toward the 45 SC threshold if much of its content falls under other criteria (e.g., Criterion 5c: professional and social topics).

Confidence Ratings

Confidence reflects how clearly a course's assignments and rubrics map to ABET's definitions — it is confidence in the credit estimate, not an assessment of the course's quality or rigor.

RatingWhat it means
High Evidence is explicit and unambiguous. Assignments use ABET-legible language — design requirements, multiple alternatives, trade-off analysis, quantitative evaluation. A reviewer is unlikely to challenge the classification.
Moderate The connection to ABET criteria is clear but requires some framing. The course may use different terminology or blend engineering content with other material, but the qualifying work is identifiable and defensible.
Low-Moderate Qualifying content exists but is partial, implicit, or relies on interpretive translation from HCD/UX framing to engineering language. Credit estimates for these courses carry more uncertainty and should be framed carefully in the self-study.
Low The ABET connection is thin or speculative. The course may contribute marginally, but a strict reviewer could reasonably exclude it. The self-study should not depend on Low-confidence courses to meet the 45 SC threshold.

Credit range bars in the dashboard use opacity to encode confidence — higher opacity indicates higher confidence in the estimate.
